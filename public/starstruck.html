CHAPTER 1

Frigid winds blew over a city blanketed in snow. The landscape glowed a faint green, reflecting the aurora lighting up the winter sky. Suddenly, the city lights flickered in warning. Like a wave, all the light across the city slowly faded and went out, causing the rosy glow of the windows to flicker out of existence.

The sudden loss of warmth from the heating system roused Sam from his sleep. The power would not be back on till morning, and he was not feeling sleepy anymore. Sam decided to take a relaxing stroll outside. He had to be as silent as possible, as his parents were sleeping in the room next door.

Throwing on his winter jacket, Sam sneaked across the house and opened the door. A cold gust of wind hit him right in the face, his reddened cheeks the evidence of the winter winds.

Stepping out of his house, Sam decided to head for the park nearby. The sky was lit with aurora, and a splattering colour of stars could be seen. Sam took a seat on a swing and stared at the sky. It was a vacation, and he did not have any classes. A few minutes passed by in silence, when someone else entered the park. A figure dressed in a thick coat could be seen, their face hidden by the darkness. From the soft glow of the moonlight, Sam could see the figure poking around at the end of the park, which was thickly planted with trees.

The figure stopped, looking around to see if they were alone. Somehow, missing Sam in the darkness, they continued what they were doing. A bright flash of light emerged from the trees, nearly blinding Sam. When he looked again, the figure had completely vanished. Thinking that they might have fallen and dropped their torch, Sam hurried to the area, but could not find anyone. He could feel something warm nearby but was not able to locate the source.

Turning on his flashlight, he pointed it at the trees, hoping to find something. The beam of light fell on the area, revealing more and more of it. Sam could see some small tracks in the snow, leading up to the middle of the woods. The tracks suddenly stopped there. A low dripping noise could be heard, the sound of snow melting. The water made its way to Sam, pooling around his feet.

Following the water upstream, Sam got to a place which felt as if it was not winter anymore. A green fog could be seen as if the aurora above had condensed into a warm fog. Cautiously, Sam took a few steps into the fog. It felt like taking a walk on a soft misty summer morning. With each step he took, the fog thickened, until the entire surrounding vanished. A gust of wind cleared the fog, allowing crystal-clear sunshine to filter in.

Sam couldn't believe it. It just felt like moments ago that it had been 4 PM. He glanced at his watch, only to be met with the display of 4:20 PM. Something was not right here. In a hurry to reach his house before his parents woke up, he turned back and ran, only to stop dead right in front of a serene seashore.

Sam stared at the sea in horror. There was no way the park was anywhere near the sea, and yet here it was, the waves crashing across the sand making a rhythmic melody. At first, Sam thought that he was hallucinating, but his watch had been working perfectly, keeping track of the time for him. There was no way this was a hallucination. Everything felt so vivid and correct.

Taking a few tentative steps towards the sea, he reached out his hand towards the water. It was cool, wrapping around his arm comfortingly. He could hear the sand crunching around him. Sam swung around and came face to face with a figure wrapped in a thick coat. Their face was hidden by their hat and scarf. Sam realized they were the same figure he had seen earlier.

"Who are you, and how did you even get here," asked the figure. "Did I not close the gate properly?"

"You are the person I saw poking around the park," said Sam.

"I was seen after all. Let me guess, you just followed me in, without even thinking about the consequences?"

An embarrassed "Yeah" was all that Sam could muster.

"That means you are stuck here for a week. I won't be able to create a gate back to your world anytime soon."

"If you can connect just my phone back to my world, I should be able to send them a message. It's winter vacations, I can just say I went on a trip or something," said Sam

"That should be possible, yes," said the figure. "Catch!" They took off their winter clothing and tossed it to Sam. Golden blond hair peeked out from underneath his hat. The figure was a guy, who appeared slightly older than Sam. He held his hands towards Sam.

Handing his phone over to the guy, Sam watched him lift it and chant something. Again, the same green glow filled the air near the phone, and Sam could see that the message he sent had gone through.

"There you go," said Jade, handing the phone back to Sam.

"Thanks," said Sam. "I am just curious but, what is this place and why did you even come to my world anyway?"

"Allow me to explain," said Jade. "I study in your world. Some people from here tend to study or work there. I came here for the winter vacation to visit my family. I am Jade, in case you were wondering"

"Wait a second, does that mean you study at my university?" said Sam

"Yeah," said Jade. "I am a senior, so that explains how we have not met each other over there. Do you see that tall structure in the distance? That is where we are going. Come along then!"

Hearing those words, Sam let himself be pulled along with wherever Jade was going. He really wanted to see how different this world was, from his home world. The structure loomed in the distance, towering over the horizon like a tree. It vaguely resembled a tree with boxy shapes attached to it.

"How long until we reach whatever that is?" asked Sam

"We should be able to reach it by tonight," said Jade. It is a week away by walk, but there are always shortcuts you know." Jade smiled at Sam. "You shall experience my world with your own eyes!"

Sam looked at his watch. Nearly six hours had passed since he first entered this world. The sky was beginning to darken, the softening of shadows heralding a change. Jade stopped Sam, pointing to a cavern.

"Here, follow me. There is a teleportation gate here, which should lead straight to my city"

Following Jade, Sam stepped into the dark cavern. A flare of light lit up behind him. Jade had done something to create a glowing orb, that was now following them around. Amazed, Sam reached out to touch the orb.

"You might not want to do that," said Jade, who was walking in front of Sam. "It will pull you in and compress you unless that's your thing"

Sam hastily pulled his hand back from the orb. "Weren't you supposed to mention this a tiny bit earlier?

"I am just joking," laughed Jade. "It just goes out when touched, see."

Jade reached out his hands and touched the orb. It vanished with a hiss.

"I think I would like the light back please," said Sam. With a flourish of Jade's hands, the orb was back up again.

Walking a bit further, they came across a large glowing gate deep inside the cavern.

"You step through this, and you are right there"

Sam reached his hands towards the gate, and slowly pulled it back without touching it. He was in a place with different rules from Earth and did not know if anything or even anyone could be trusted.

"Don't worry. This just directly takes you to the city, with no chance of failure. Hold my hand. I shall go in first" Jade held out his hand to Sam. Reaching out, Sam took the offered hand. Jade stepped into the gate, smoothly dragging Sam to the other side.

It was a giant tree all along. Sam stared at the sky trying to gauge the tree's height, but the tree seemed to go on forever. There were many residences and a giant fortress, all constructed right into the tree. The rest of the city sprawled from the base of the tree, and far away into the distance.

"Welcome to the citadel of Kanara, my hometown and the capital city of Aquaria!"

CHAPTER 2

The first time Jade saw Sam, he was standing on the beach, wandering around aimlessly. Jade realized that someone had followed him and decided to take him back to his city so that he could be sent back as soon as possible. Jade did not know what the immigration officials would say, but he hoped that Sam could return without any trouble.

Jade glanced at Sam, who was staring at the giant fortress, entranced. He felt a glimmer of pride at his hometown being appreciated. The second they entered the city a group of officials was waiting for them.

"Looks like they did sense you enter after all," said Jade. "Just follow along with them. I shall take care of the explanation."

The officials led them to the giant tree. The base level was a lounge room, which had a bunch of warp gates set up along the far end. Each of the warp gates led to a different level in the tree. Many people were busy going to different levels through the gates. The officials walked to a gate and motioned for them to enter.

Sam held out his hand to Jade. Grabbing hold of Sam's hand, Jade entered the warp gate behind the officers, pulling Sam along, just like earlier. Jade was met with the familiar room of the transfer office, from when he first applied for a transfer to Earth. The officials made them sit and started with their questioning.

"So, how did you end up here anyway?" asked the transfer head.

"I saw a green glow and went to explore. That somehow transported me here," replied Sam.

"Didn't you confirm that no one else was there nearby?" This time, the question was directed at Jade.

"I did," said Jade. "It was 2 AM, and I did not expect anyone to be around. Not like our world's magic works there anyway, apart from the warp device you gave." Jade took out a small cube from his pocket and handed it to the head.

"Yeah, this device will take a week to charge up again," said the Head. "Is your friend over there in any hurry to return? If so, we can try finding another device that is charged."

"Nope," replied Sam. "I already sent a message to my parents, so I won't be missed for a week."

"That's good then," said the Head. "Just fill this out, and we can clear you soon enough." The Head handed Sam a form.

Jade peeked at the form. It was a generic personal details declaration form. Sam started filling in the details one by one. So that's his full name, Sam Forger, Jade noted.

Jade was shooed away by Sam, so he went back to looking around the office. The Head was missing. It looked like the Head had gone to a storeroom behind the office. After a while, the Head returned with a strange wristband-like object. Jade could sense the magic coming off the wristband.

"If you have finished the form, please keep it over there," said the Head.

Jade watched as Sam put the completed form on the table and eyed the object in the Head's hand.

"What is this for?" asked Sam.

"This is a system to keep track of otherworld visitors," said the Head, handing over the wristband to Sam. "Keep it on at all times, unless you want to be imprisoned."

Sam handed the wristband to Jade. "Do you think this is safe?"

"I have not seen one of these in use, as no one from your world visits, but I have heard of such tracking systems being used," replied Jade. "It's probably like the passport system of your world."

Sam reached out his hand to take the wristband, but Jade deftly slipped the wristband on Sam.

"Nice accessory you have there," said Jade with a smirk. "Hold on, let me make it look like a normal watch." Waving his hand over the wristband, it now started looking like an innocent watch. "Just an illusion, but it looks better, I suppose."

Sam glanced down at his hand, which had doubled in the number of watches. "Now I have two watches, huh?"

***

Heading out of the office, Jade turned to Sam. "Now that you're in the clear, it is time to visit my home. I live somewhere over there," said Jade, pointing vaguely to a location beyond the great tree. The streets were clear and lit with brightly glowing yellow orbs, outlining a tall wall visible in the distance. "Follow me!"

Jade walked along with Sam, pointing out different parts of the city to him. "The entire area from the base of the tree to that wall over there is the fortress of Kanara. And that is where we can exit the fortress," he said, nodding towards a gate looming in front of them. The gate was still open, allowing people in and out. "My house is just beyond the fortress."

"That is one giant gate," remarked Sam. Jade noticed Sam looking at the fortress, trying to drink in all the sights of the city. "Thank you for showing me all this, Jade."

"It gets better," laughed Jade. "You must see how beautiful the great tree looks in the morning. Oh, and the view from the top floors is just amazing, you know?"

Telling the tales of his city to Sam, they continued their way to Jade's home. After a while, Jade stopped in front of a tall apartment building. Entering it, he led the way to an apartment on the 6th floor.

"Damn, this looks like an expensive apartment block," said Sam, looking around the house. The modern feel of the house seemed to impress him. "So, this is where we will be staying?"

"Yup, my parents don't stay here, so it's fine." Jade pointed to a room. "You take that guest room, and I shall stay in mine. If at any point you feel scared, feel free to wake me up."

"Thanks again, Jade," said Sam, going over to the room and opening it. It was nice and spacious, with a light orb casting the room with a soft glow. Sam leapt face-first on the bed. "I think I will be able to sleep peacefully!"

Leaving Sam to sleep, Jade retired to his room and sat at his desk. He was unable to sleep. It had been half a year since he came back to his house, not that he wanted to come here anyway. The empty house was a stark reminder of the fact that his parents had abandoned him a few years back. They had divorced and left the house to him before running off to different cities.

Ever since that day, he had worked hard to apply for the transfer program so that he could leave the memory behind. Being back here gave him a strange feeling. Thankfully, some of his friends from college were from his world, so there was a chance they might visit. Then there was Sam as well, sleeping in the next room, so the house did not feel empty anymore.

Walking back to Sam's room, Jade knocked. "Are you awake Sam?"

"Yeah," a voice replied. "I am just processing the events of the day. A lot of things happened huh?"

Walking into the room, Jade sat on a chair, which was by the bed.

"Where are your parents anyway? This house is way too big for a person living here alone"

"They have moved on to their different paths" Jade turned his face away from Sam. "Guess they got tired of playing house"

"I'm sorry for asking"

"Nah, it's fine. I have had a long time to come to terms with it anyway. Rest well then, we have a lot to see tomorrow!"

CHAPTER 3

The next day was bright and sunny, leading Jade and Sam to wake up early. It was time for sightseeing at last. Sam couldn't wait to see the city view from the higher levels of the great tree. He was glad he somehow ended up here and met Jade. He just hoped that Jade wouldn't be punished somehow for letting an outsider like him into this world.

Sam could hear a soft bubbling noise coming up nearby. He followed the source of the sound to find Jade preparing breakfast. It wasn't anything like preparing breakfast on Earth. Jade was waving his hands and chanting something. Food just started moving around, getting chopped, and getting cooked all on its own.

"How do you like it?" asked Jade. "This is how we cook here, very easy! We can't make the food itself appear with our powers, but it can be cooked in an instant."

"Amazing! Is there any way I can learn such powers?"

"You have to be born in this world," said Jade. "I do vaguely remember hearing about some ways for outsiders though." Jade's face turned mildly pink. "Something about binding?"

"Binding, hmm." Sam walked up to the food and sniffed it. "Smells good!"

After eating a pleasant and filling breakfast, Sam set out with Jade to see the famous tourist sites of the city. There were not many people this early in the morning, so the city was quite calm. As Jade knew a lot about this city, he was able to provide a quick answer to any questions that Sam asked. After walking back into the fortress, Jade made his way to the lounge of the great tree and beckoned Sam.

"You wanted to see the view from the top, right? Well, we really can't reach the treetop since no one has scaled it yet, but the observatory floor should be reachable easily using the warp gates."

Jade led Sam to a gate titled "Observatory Tower." Below it was a notice, "Climb with care." Stepping in, they were faced with a room, which had a set of stairs leading up above them.

"All these advanced magical technologies and there are stairs?" Sam looked at the stairs, puzzled.

"I suppose it is there for the vibes," said Jade. "Go on then."

Climbing carefully behind Jade, Sam went ahead. The stairs seemed to keep going on forever, going round and round in circles up a small chute. After what seemed like a century, the stairs finally led to an exit. It was a glass room directly attached to the side of the tree.

Sam shakily stepped into the glass room. Below it stretched the entirety of the city of Kanara. The light of the morning sun bathed the city in golden-blue hues. Entranced, Sam went to the edge of the observatory and stared at the city below. Jade came up behind him and stood right next to him.

"Cool, right! Many years back, my father took me here for the first time. The view now is just as stunning as it was back then. The city did expand quite a bit, though."

Sam continued looking at the city below, trying to spot something interesting. Far beyond the city outskirts, he could see the beginnings of something dark, something that was not lit by the rising sun.

"What is that dark place over there?" asked Sam. "It looks like a forest of some kind."

"Oh, that is the cursed forest," said Jade casually. "It protects this city from any invasions, but yeah, if you get stuck in there, you are not making it back at all."

"Then how do people even leave this city anyway?"

"Teleport gate is the key," said Jade. "There is a gate at the outskirts of the city that bypasses the forest entirely. Supposedly, someone found a way through the forest, so they just set up a teleport gate once they got here. Want me to take you a bit inside it?"

Sam shuddered. "No thanks, I don't want to get lost in there forever."

"No need to worry about that," said Jade. "I still have the warp gate, so we can use that to get out. My friends will come here tomorrow, so we can all go with them on a sightseeing trip."

Trying to balance his curiosity and his fear, Sam considered Jade's words. "If you can get us back, then it's fine, I suppose."

Sam reached out to his phone to take some pictures, but Jade stopped his hand.

"Please don't," said Jade. "In case this picture ends up in your world's network, it will cause a big problem. Go ahead and use my phone. It is modified to be protected from external threats."

Jade handed his phone to Sam. Throwing his arm over Jade, Sam held the phone in front of them. "Smile!" With a click, the shutter fired, capturing the picture of the duo with the city sprawling behind them.

***

By the time they left the observatory, it was almost lunchtime. Many other people had started coming up to the observatory to view the city below them. Sam's stomach grumbled, so he turned to Jade.

"Know any good places to eat, Jade?"

"That is where I am heading," teased Jade. "I could feel the hunger radiating off you."

Sam's face flushed. "I shall be ready to eat then."

They walked up to a beautiful villa-like restaurant, stepped inside, and approached the counter.

"The usual, please," said Jade. "For the two of us."

Heading to the table, which was just cleared by the hotel staff, they took a seat. The aroma of the cooking food wafted through the open kitchen door, filling the room with an expectant smell.

"This place smells really good," said Sam. "What does the usual consist of?"

"You'll see," said Jade. "All I shall say is, it will be the best food you have ever eaten."

Just then, the restaurant staff came over to the table, carrying dishes of varying colours. They placed everything on the table in a neat fashion.

"Enjoy your food, sirs."

The food did not look like anything from Earth. It was different and had completely different fruits and meat that did not exist on Earth. Sam looked at the food and hesitated.

"Are you sure I can eat this? Seeing as I am from Earth, after all."

"Yeah, it's fine, don't worry. Your world's food does not do anything to us, so you should be fine too," said Jade. "Go on then."

Taking his spoon, Sam dipped it into a randomly selected plate with strange meat and brought the food up to his mouth. A pleasant smell immediately assaulted his nostrils. Upon taking a bite, Sam discovered it tasted amazing. It reminded him of high-end cooked meat from back home. Immediately turning his attention to his other plates, he made quick work of it. Jade, who was watching him eat with a strange expression on his face, handed him a glass of water, barely able to suppress his laughter.

"I told you it's the best," said Jade. "I come here whenever I feel like treating myself. These guys really know how to cook."

After a while, Jade finished up his plate and called the staff for the bill. Sam went forward to pay but realized his currency was of no use in this world.

"Oh, don't feel bad, it's all transfer program money anyway. Feel free to treat me when the next semester starts."

"Of course," said Sam. "I know of some amazing places back home myself."

"I shall look forward to it then."

Leaving the restaurant, they walked back to Jade's house. Sam remembered Jade saying something about his friends arriving later that day.

"You said your friends will be visiting?"

"Yeah," replied Jade. "They will be coming to the warp port in the evening. They sent me a message a while back."

Jade handed Sam an open letter. Something was written there in a script that was illegible to Sam.

"Your language seems pretty interesting," said Sam. "When were you sent the letter anyway?"

"I just received it right now," said Jade. "We don't have smartphones here, but messages can be sent by writing a letter and transferring it directly to the person, wherever they may be. Look at this."

Jade took out a sheet of paper and handed it to Sam. "Write a reply. English is fine, they are transfer students too, but not from our university."

Jade handed Sam a pen, with which he wrote a reply to the letter. Handing the letter back to Jade, Sam watched keenly as Jade waved his hand, and the letter disappeared.

"There we go. They should have got the reply now."

As the day passed, they lounged around the house, with Sam asking Jade many questions about the world. Finally, it was almost time for Jade's friends to arrive. Sending his friends a message to confirm the timings once again, they slowly made their way to the warp port.

Upon arriving at the warp port, Sam observed a lot of people coming and going from the city. There was a small general check-in process right after entering, but it was not as extensive as the ones back on Earth. Jade was intently staring at the warp gate, waiting for his friends to come. After some time, Jade pointed to a group of people. Sam could see two girls and a guy making their way through the check-in and heading straight for Jade.

"There they are! Those are the people who I did my schooling with. All transfer students, as I said earlier. Sam, meet Ryan, Emily, and Lydia!"

CHAPTER 4

At long last, Jade's friends had arrived. Jade could feel their thoughts accusing him. After all, he had broken one of the most important transfer student rules.

"Let me guess, you saw Jade coming back here?" said Ryan. "And managed to follow him?" Ryan held his hand towards Sam, who was staring at them uncertainly. "I am Ryan, a friend of Jade. We all were in the same transfer program batch."

Shaking his hand, Sam introduced himself to the others. Seeing things going along smoothly, Jade sighed a breath of relief.

"So that's the tracking device huh," said Lydia, noticing the band on Sam's hands.

"Yeah," said Jade. "Seems it acts like a visa or something"

"I see, so Jade, how was your past semester" questioned Lydia. "Met any new people? Made any new friends? You always had trouble talking to people. More importantly, finally met any girl you wanted to date?" Lydia pinned Jade with her eyes.

Jade pointed at Sam. "This is the answer to the first part of your question. He is from my university. And for the second part of your question, my answer remains the same."

Emily burst out laughing. "You are the same as ever Jade."

Sam, who was chatting with Ryan about his university, turned to the girls. "Yes, he has a new friend now, and I shall make him talk to as much as new people as possible. In fact, I shall arrange for him to meet one new guy or girl per day, his choice"

Jade noticed Sam was good at interacting with people. Sam could freely talk to the people he had just met. Picking up a piece of paper, he crumpled it and tossed it accurately at Sam. "Hey, enough of that!"

Laughing, the group slowly left the warp port and headed back to Jade's house. After settling their luggage at his house, they decided to go to sleep for the night. Jade's house had three rooms. Ryan and Emily were given their own room, as they were dating. This left Lydia with her own room if Sam shared Jade's.

"Oh, come on, you know I don't like staying alone in a room, Jade," said Lydia. "Usually, me and Emily share the room."

Jade thought about this for a bit. "Then, would you like to stay with Sam and me? Someone must sleep on the floor in that case."

"Yes, please" in an instant, Lydia headed straight to Jade's room. Jade sat on his bed and was going to assign Lydia to the floor when Sam spoke up.

"I shall take the floor, no worries," said Sam. "You have a foldable mattress, after all, Jade"

Jade could see the happiness radiating off Lydia's face.

"Thanks a lot, Sam!" said Lydia, and started heading for the bed, but Jade stopped her. Jade could not allow this to happen. He had always known that Lydia liked him, but he could not give a concrete answer to her feelings. With all the things that happened in his family, he was very afraid of dating someone.

"It's fine Sam, Lydia will take the floor. You are a guest to this world. I can't have you sleeping on the floor, can I?

A flash of annoyance passed Lydia's face. "Yes, you are right Jade, I shall take the floor"

Jade could feel Sam's confusion. After all, Sam would have thought he was doing them a favour. They all settled in for the night, but Jade was unable to sleep as usual. He decided to take a breather on the balcony. The night sky was as beautiful as ever. In this world, it was much more colourful and vivid, than back on earth.

"Can't sleep again huh?" Jade heard a voice behind him. It was Sam, coming up to check on him.

"Sorry for waking you up"

"It's fine, I was just going to ask, what is up between you and Lydia anyway? I got the feeling she liked you, so I offered the bed"

"It's complicated," said Jade. "It's not like I dislike her, but I don't know, I just can't date anyone right now."

"Then why don't u reject her directly? Or at least let her know"

"It's not like she confessed to me" continued Jade. "Besides, I don't want to ruin my friend's dynamics. Rejecting her will make things awkward between us."

"I see," said Sam, simply "Just tell her when you are ready"

Jade liked that about Sam. He was not the kind of guy to judge someone, based on their decisions. He just accepted them for what they were.

"Sure will, one day"

***

The next day was bright and sunny. It was the perfect day to explore the cursed forest. The name "cursed" was a bit of an exaggeration. It was a famous tourist spot in that city. It was safe if the established roads and fences were followed. Jade woke up to the sounds of something cooking. He made his way to the kitchen and found Sam cooking. Lydia was nearby, measuring out the ingredients.

"It's time for breakfast, Jade!" Said Sam. "I know how to cook, but the ingredients here are so different, so I had Lydia help me out with the recipes"

Once the entire group woke up, Sam started serving breakfast. Jade found the breakfast to be amazing. Sam was a great cook.

"So how is it, Jade?" asked Sam.

"It is really amazing." Said Jade. "Especially this fried stuff, see?" Jade forked a piece of the meat and brought it to Sam's face. "Try it for yourself"

Laughing, Sam ate the piece. "Thank Lydia too, she was such a help with the recipe"

"Yes, thank me too, Jade," said Lydia with a smile. "Somehow the food turned out really well heh"

"You did well too, Lydia"

After everyone finished their food, they started on their way to the forest. As it was not a weekend, there was barely any crowd at the location. Jade immediately pulled Sam along to the stalls, which were selling snacks. Lydia decided to stick with him, while Ryan and Emily decided to start exploring the forest a bit ahead of the others.

"That couple is going on a date huh," remarked Lydia, staring at her friends slowly going deeper into the forest. "We won't be able to see them for the rest of the visit, that's for sure."

"They told us to meet up at the clearing," said Jade. "They won't ditch us, don't worry."

"It's not that, never mind," said Lydia. "Want to explore a bit, while we wait for Sam to finish his food?"

"That guy will surely get into trouble if we leave him alone," said Jade. "He will be finished soon."

With a pout, Lydia turned and started walking towards the food stalls. "Think I am going to get something to eat too."

After they were done, they started going deeper into the forest. It was pretty, with a variety of different animals and birds, none of them found on earth. After walking for a while, Jade pointed out a fence made up of bushes to Sam.

"Beyond this, are the unmapped areas," said Jade. "If you cross them, and wander off, you won't be able to come back, unless you remember the exact way you took."

"Not like it's even crossable," said Sam. "Just look at how thick and high the bushes are."

"It's pretty easy for us, I will show you in a while"

The group slowly made their way to the clearing, taking in the sights of the forest along the way. The second they reached the clearing, they could see Ryan and Emily coming up to meet them, seemingly out of breath.

"Made it here just in time, huh," said Lydia. "Almost thought you guys won't show up"

"We promised, Dia," said Emily. "Anyway, onwards then!"

"What do you mean, onwards? Asked Sam. "Looks like the fences say otherwise."

"You really think some fences would stop our Jade here?" smirked Lydia. "Just watch"

Jade circled the clearing, trying to find the spot, which he had found back in his school days. It was his secret location, which he had usually gone to when he was feeling down. After a bit of searching, Jade stopped near a spot and raised his hand.

"From the bottom of the earth, the trees grow and die" chanted Jade. Immediately, the bushes parted, to make a clearing for someone to pass through. Jade motioned for his friends to follow, and they all slowly started walking in a single file behind him.

"It has been quite a while since I stabilized this path," said Jade. "Hopefully, it still leads to our secret base."

"The locations tend to shift around then?" asked Sam.

"Yeah" replied Jade. "Beyond the barrier, there are no fixed maps or locations. It all depends on having a stabilized path to certain locations. If you don't leave this path, you should be fine."

After following the path for a bit, the forest slowly started thinning out. They could smell the crispy sea breeze right up ahead. The path led them directly to the top of the cliffs. The evening sun lit up the cliffs with a golden glow. Jade could see their base, which they had constructed back in their school days. It was still standing, to Jade's surprise.

"Is that the secret base you guys have been talking about? Looks amazing"

"Yeah, we built it during one vacation," said Ryan. "We usually stay here during our vacations. Come take a look inside!"
