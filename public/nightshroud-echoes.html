CHAPTER 1

Introduction Arc

The evening sunlight cast a warm glow, illuminating the farmlands surrounding the city of Ansphin. The sun was about to set, and Kieran and his younger sister, Kiara, had to get back into the city before the trains stopped service. Kieran's family were the ones who managed the food supplies for the city, so they had to come out to the farmlands every week and oversee the loading of all the supplies into the train, which delivered it directly to Ansphin.

Kieran watched as the workers loaded the last few boxes into the train. All he had to do was to sign the completion form, before he could finally head home. Kieran let his gaze drift around the golden farmlands, watching the cornstalks sway in the breeze. The city had no place like this, only endless metal, and stone all around.

The area beyond the farmlands were extremely dangerous for people, and were filled with dangerous creatures walking around. A few years back, Kieran once tried checking what was out there out of pure curiosity, but the second he crossed the barrier, a barren wasteland stretched endlessly before him. He wandered around for a bit, till a loud roar shattered the silence, convincing him to turn back.

There were some people having some strange powers, who could survive the wasteland, but they were very elusive, and rarely appeared in public. Kieran wanted to join them so bad, but he did not possess any powers at all as far as he knew, and neither did his sister.

Kieran sighed, shaking himself from his thoughts. The boxes were done being loaded, and he needed to go and check on his sister. Turning away from the train station, Kieran headed to a small office that was built right next to it. This was where Kiara handled payments. She was just finishing up when he walked in.

"Are all the boxes done being loaded?" asked Kiara, casually signing some documents that was being handed over to her by some farmers.

"Yeah, it just got over" replied Kieran. "Just get the payments settled, and we can head back home. I am so tired, and I want to catch up with that new show that was supposed to release today."

"Yeah, yeah, just a bit more." Said Kiara. "Oh, the show is releasing today, right? Almost forgot about that, we shall watch it together when we get back."

Kiara slowly finished up the payments, and Kieran started walking towards the train station. Right when he was about to reach the train station, a sharp flash of light interrupted his vision. It seemed to come from a bit further ahead into the farmlands.

"Did you see that too, Ki?" asked Kieran, turning around.

"Yeah, I did" said Kiara, who had been walking a bit behind him, looking up something on her phone. "Do you want to go check it out?"

"How do to plan to get back home then?" said Kieran. "The train will be leaving soon, and it's a long walk to the city from this far out."

"It's fine, we can just stay at an inn here till the morning train." said Kiara. "Just inform the workers, and we shall go check it out."

After Kieran was done with the final checks, he and his sister watched as the train slowly departed the station, loaded to the brim with food. The train slowly grew smaller and smaller as it disappeared beyond the horizon. The sky had completely turned dark, and the landscape before him was lit only by the silver glimmer of moonlight.

One good thing about the darkness was that it made it easy to spot light flashes. Kieran barely had to wait before there was another flash of light coming from the general direction of the previous flash.

Kiara, who was scanning the environment intently, quickly started running towards the flash of light "Hurry up, we need to catch up before they leave"

Kieran started running after his sister. He wasn't sure if the flashes were anything that important, but he did want to check it out in case it was something interesting. There was a semi forested area, and it looked like they had to cross it to get there.

"Imagine it is just some random farmer and his torchlight" said Kieran, taking out his phone to check the map. "It looks like we are nearly at the barrier"

"Come on, where is your sense of adventure" Kiara nudged Kieran with her elbow. "There is no way a farmer's torch can make flashes like that"

"We shall see for ourselves soon enough I guess"

As they kept running, the trees slowly started thinning. Ahead of him, Kieran could see a small clearing come into view.

"We need to walk carefully from now on" said Kieran, slowing his pace to reduce the noise that he made. "We don't want to alert whoever or whatever is there"

Kiara nodded, carefully looking around her. "Are we still within the barrier, or did we cross it?"

"We are still inside the barrier," said Kieran. "The barrier is just past that clearing according to this map."

Carefully making sure to stay hidden under the shadows cast by the trees, Kieran and his sister slipped through the trees, their footsteps muffled by the undergrowth. The clearing ahead was bathed in glowing moonlight, straight out of a fairytale.

Right in the middle of the clearing was a lone figure, barely visible, even with the moonlight. They definitely were not a farmer, as far as Kieran could tell. The figure did not move, as if waiting for something to happen. A few seconds later, a giant creature blinked into existence, heading straight for the lone figure.

Kieran froze, turning sharply to Kiara. "That's bad, we need to get away from here before it notices us"

"Yeah, we need to report this once we get back. I did not know the creatures could cross the barrier"

Kieran grabbed his sister, and just when he was about to start running, a feminine voice floated towards him like a warm summer's rain.

"You guys are not as hidden as you think you are, you know," said the girl. Kieran turned towards the clearing, watching the girl slowly walk towards him. The monster was nowhere to be seen. Did she defeat the monster all on her own? Kieran had no clue. At least they did not have to worry about the monster anymore.

"Who are you?" asked Kieran, staring at the girl, who was now close enough to be visible. Her face was still partially covered by her hoodie. He was not sure if they could trust her, but all he could do in the moment was to keep the conversation going, and slowly plan his retreat.

In response, the girl lowered her hoodie, letting locks of brown hair spill past her shoulders. As her face was fully revealed, Kieran found himself momentarily taken aback. She really was cute, with striking features that suited her confidence.

"I am Elena, one of the members of the Eclipse Seekers, Division 5"

'And what about the monster you just fought?" asked Kiara, looking around the clearing for any signs of ambush.

"Oh, don't worry about that," said Elena. "It's just a manifestation of my power, and won't harm anyone."

"That means you guys are from the group which my brother Kieran wanted to join" Kiara turned to Kieran and smirked. "Unfortunately, he does not have any powers, and neither do I."

"Wait a second, did you just say Kieran? Is your name Kiara by any chance?"

Kiara's surprise was visible on her face. Kieran had no idea how did someone from a mysterious group knew their names.

"Yes, my name is Kiara, and that's Kieran" replied Kiara, with a guarded voice. "How do you even know about us anyway?"

"Uhm, this is bit of a secret, and I am sure I will get into trouble for telling you guys this, but you were actually on the list to be recruited into our organization. Elena stared at the ground, seemingly lost in thought. "I don't know the full story as I am a relatively new member myself but yeah, you guys are meant to be with us."

"So, you are saying we have powers then?" said Kieran. There was no way he had any powers. When he was younger, he wished desperately to be able to go beyond the barrier and do something useful for the city, but as he grew older, he realized that nothing can change the fact that he is powerless, so he had cast away his dreams.

"Yeah, it is just dormant, and can be awakened," said Elena. "Actually, it seems like majority of the population have the power, but it is usually too weak to even be of use for most people. Anyway, I should get you guys to our headquarters, before the other organizations try to recruit you."

"Why have we not been contacted by other organizations at all? Asked Kiara.

His sister was right, Kieran had never been contacted by anyone claiming to have powers like this at all.

"No clue," replied Elena. "I just heard that all such organizations who form a collection of people like us, were banned from ever contacting you."

"Ah you are going to be in huge trouble then" said Kiara, displaying a warm smile. "Shall we go with Elena and see what happens, Kieran?"

Kieran could not think of any objections. He wanted to get the full truth behind whatever is happening, and he knew the only way for that was to go along with Elena, and question the organization directly. "Not like you are going to listen if I say otherwise Ki"

"You are right about that!" laughed Kiara, heading after Elena.

Kieran hesitated a bit before stepping forward. There was no turning back now, he needed to know how to use his powers, that was his dream after all.

CHAPTER 2

"Hey Elena, how do you plan to get back to your headquarters?" asked Kieran. "Going to the city is impossible this late at night"

"Our headquarters is pretty close by actually," said Elena. "It should be about an hour of walking from here"

"No way, if it was located here, we would have definitely seen it," said Kiara. "We come here every week to load supplies from different parts of the farmlands."

"You will be surprised" Elena smiled. "Come, it's just a little further now"

Kieran slowly made his way towards the location of the headquarters following Elena. He had no idea how a set of buildings can be hidden that easily. Kieran could not see anything resembling any headquarters. Verdant horizons stretched endlessly before him, lit by the silver moonlight. It had been nearly an hour since they started walking, and they did not reach anything yet.

Kiara turned back towards Kieran, giving him a questioning look. "I am pretty sure we have come in this general location, and absolutely nothing was here."

"Yeah, I wonder how they hide their headquarters"

"We have reached the headquarters" announced Elena. "Now watch me carefully as I show you the entrance."

Elena stepped forward, resting her hands against an invisible wall. Kieran tried walking towards the wall, but Elena extended her hand towards him, blocking his way.

"Don't do that" warned Elena. "It's an illusion barrier, which will confuse your sense of surroundings, if you are not integrated with a Nightshroud crystal."

Kieran jumped back, keeping a healthy distance from the barrier. Elena walked around for a while, till she stopped in her tracks, her hands still on the barrier.

"Found the switch," said Elena, flipping the switch. "This will disable the barrier, so that you two can come inside"

The entire barrier rippled, and slowly faded out of existence. An entire sea of buildings stood in the place where previously there was nothing. It was a sight to behold indeed. Kieran could not see anyone nearby currently. The entire place was lit up by soft yellow street lights, similar to the ones in the city. Kiara similarly was staring at the city in surprise.

"Welcome to The Eclipse Seekers!" said Elena, giving Kieran and his sister a proud smile. "How do you like it?"

"I did not expect this at all," said Kiara. "I though you would take a portal or something to the headquarter."

"Unfortunately, no one knows how to make portal anymore," said Elena. "The ones in the city are just linked to the few cities near us, as you might know."

Kieran had seen the 4 portals that people used to travel between the linked cities, but he and Kiara were not allowed to travel outside the city by their parents.

"Now what do we do? Asked Kieran. "This place is barely guarded for such a secret headquarter."

"That is how much they trust the barrier," said Elena. "I shall put it back up, and we shall head to my accommodation for the night. I don't want to get yelled at by the leaders right now, so we shall deal with it tomorrow."

Kieran would have normally hesitated going to a girl's room this late at night but thankfully, his sister was there to prevent any awkwardness. Noticing the look on his face, Kiara turned towards him. He instantly realised that Kiara being here was the worst possible thing to happen.

"Feeling shy are we, Kieran?"

"Oh no, nothing like that" Kieran casually moved beside Kiara, pulling her away from Elena. "It's getting late, we need to be well rested before tomorrow"

Elena, who was watching the exchange just smiled. "Kieran is right, let's get going!"

Elena started heading towards her house. Kieran turned towards his sister and whispered. "I shall get you back for that, you wait"

Laughing, Kiara started walking towards Elena, leaving Kieran to slowly take in the sights of the city. There were many different buildings located there, and many of them were residential buildings. The main feature of the area was the guild headquarters, which Kieran could see in the distance. It stretched above the other buildings, standing tall amidst the smaller buildings.

After a short walk, Elena stopped in front of a small building. It was a small apartment style building.

"Here we are," said Elena. "Make yourselves at home"

"Damn, its much bigger than I expected," said Kieran, taking a quick look at the building. It looked old, but also cozy in a sense.

"Even new recruits get houses like this?" continued Kiara.

"Yeah, since we barely have any members," said Elena, as she entered her house, and switched on the lights. A warm glow illuminated the space as light flooded the area. The apartment consisted of a living room, with a small bedroom at the side. "The top leaders have much bigger houses, closer to the guild headquarters, but most members live in places like this."

"Looks like being a member comes with some amazing perks indeed!" said Kiara, as she took in the surroundings.

"The dangers balance it out" Elena let out a short laugh, but Kieran noticed the way her fingers tightened against the mattress she was holding. "Anyway, I have pulled out these spare beds for you guys. If you need anything, just call me, I shall be in my room."

Kiara gave Elena a small nod. "Thanks, Elena. You should get some rest too."

"Goodnight then, Elena" said Kieran, heading for the bed. He was exhausted from all the walking, but he was curious to know about what really was this organization, and what Elena meant by dangers. He would be finding out soon enough.

***

Morning sunlight streamed into the room, waking up Kieran from his sleep. Kiara was still sleeping. She always used to wake up pretty late, and it was Kieran's everyday routine dragging her out of bed for their work.

"Up already huh? We need to get going then" said Elena, who was sitting on the couch, reading something.

"Kiara is not going to wake up anytime soon," said Kieran, heading over to Kiara and giving her a shake. "Wake up, we have stuff to do"

Kiara jumped out of her bed, and without a pause, folded her mattress to put it away, leaving Kieran in stunned silence.

"That's a record breaking wake up time right there Ki," said Kieran, putting away his own mattress too.

"Yeah, we have a busy day ahead of us, you know."

With a resigned shake of his head, Kieran got ready to leave. Once they had finished with the pack of instant noodles provided by Elena, they started heading out towards the headquarters. There were many people out and about, and quite a few of them cast curious glances at Kieran and his sister.

"I am pretty sure news about you guys would have reached the headquarters," said Elena. "Just explain yourselves calmly and you should be fine"

The main building of the headquarters loomed before Kieran, tall and imposing. It was of a similar design to the city square, just a lot bigger. Elena went ahead of him, and cracked open the door, revealing the insides of the hall. It was ornately decorated, with huge pillars and designs everywhere. At the end of the hall, there were four figures standing, glaring at Elena with a sharp look. It looked like they were not happy to see Kieran and his sister there at all.

"Elena, may I know what the Solvaris siblings are doing here" said a figure in the middle, his voice laced with anger. He was wearing a robe the shade of the setting sun.

"I am truly sorry Captain Tianxi." Said Elena, carefully staring at the floor. "They ended up catching me practicing my fighting out in the fields"

"All you had to do is ignore them, and move on" Tianxi sighed, turning his gaze to Kieran and Kiara. "Please forget about us. If we let you guys in, your parents will make sure to shut this organization down."

"Our parents? They are not anywhere near the city," said Kiara. "They said they had something important to do, and went away about half a year back. There is no way they will even bother shutting you down, just because we are here."

Tianxi's face was a portrait of surprise. "Wait, that does not sound like them, are you guys in contact at least?"

"They message once in a while" replied Kieran. "Enough about that, are you guys letting us in or not?"

"Not formally, that will get all the other organizations after us" said Tianxi. "We do have a better idea, since Elena was the one who brought you guys here, she will be assigned to train you as punishment. Make sure to keep them safe Elena."

Elena took a deep bow. "I shall make sure to protect them till they can use their Nightshroud powers properly, Captain Tianxi."

Captain Tianxi headed towards Kieran, and handed him a spherical object. It looked like a cloud was swirling inside it, giving off a faint blue glow. He handed a similar glowing sphere to Kiara too.

"Elena will teach you how to use it" said Tianxi. "You may use an empty apartment as your base. We will assign one close to Elena's. You are dismissed"

"One last question" interjected Kieran. "Who were my parents even?"

Tianxi and the other leaders just gave Kieran a soft look before turning away. "Nope, we have no idea who they are. Please be on your way now."

Giving them a quick bow, Kieran slowly started making his way for the exit. It was clear they were hiding something, but he had no idea how to get the truth from them.

CHAPTER 3

Kieran was seated at a restaurant table, across Elena and Kiara. They had headed straight to a restaurant that Elena recommended, so he was treating them to lunch. His Nightshroud crystal was still tightly held in his hands. Kieran brought it closer, watching the clouds of blue swirl and flow within it.

"So, this will unlock our powers huh?"

"A part of it, yeah," said Elena. "A blue crystal is used just for basic protection. If you want to see true power, you will have to find yourself a purple or red one."

"And they did not have any red crystals at the headquarter to give us?" said Kiara, her face an expression of disbelief.

"Nope" replied Elena, her face darkening "The last time we tried to get a red one went very badly. We just don't have enough people here to handle multiple Nightshroud of that level."

Kieran quickly changed the topic on catching her expression. He then started asking her about the other members who were a part of the Eclipse Seekers.

"Our organization is barely holding on compared to the ones in other cities," said Elena. "I'm the only blue crystal user in our age group, the vast majority of them are just green crystals."

"Why don't the leaders just give out a blue crystal to everyone like they did to us?" said Kieran. Something did not seem right about just being given a blue crystal that easily.

"I think, for most people, anything higher than a green crystal just gets rejected by their bodies instantly," said Elena. "I was given a green crystal at first, but upgraded to a blue crystal that I found during that raid I mentioned earlier."

"So, what about us? We have a blue crystal," said Kiara. "You think it will get rejected?"

"The leaders think that you guys should be fine starting off with a blue crystal," said Elena. "They know what they are doing I suppose. Anyway, we shall see how well you integrate once we get the process started."

Once they were done with their food, Elena took them straight to an arena, which Kieran recalled seeing on his way to the headquarters. There were already a few people in there, most of them looked a bit older than him.

A guy with soft black hair and brown eyes glanced over as they approached. Behind him stood a brown-haired guy and a silver-haired girl with striking green eyes.

"Hey Elena, who are those people with you" The black-haired guy asked. "The girl is such a cutie; can I date her?"

Kiara let out a quiet laugh, clearly entertained, but Kieran wasn't as amused. "Please do not flirt with my sister."

Before the guy could respond, the brown-haired guy sighed and reached over, giving his ear a sharp tug. "Ignore Jin. He flirts with everything that moves,"

Shaking her head, Elena replied. "It's Kieran and Kiara, I think should explain everything." Turning to Kieran and Kiara, she gestured towards the trio. "These are Jin, Cillian, and Isla. They have been members for a long time now."

Isla whistled. "No wonder the leaders were cranky today when we met them. You managed to recruit them huh?"

"Well, not formally," said Elena. "We are supposed to keep them associated with the Eclipse Seekers, so they assigned a new member like me to train them."

Kieran decided to try his luck getting information out of these members. They all were talking about him and Kiara as if they were some important people, but the leaders did not answer at all.

Kieran turned his gaze towards Isla. "I asked the leaders this, and got no reply. Who are my parents anyway?"

"See, from what I heard, they were bigshots around these parts," said Isla. "Don't know anything more than that though."

"Yeah, that was obvious enough, seeing the way we are being treated," said Kiara. "So even you guys don't know anything else huh?"

"I shall ask the leaders for more information if you want," said Isla. "I shall update you if I learn anything new"

Giving her a quick nod, Kiara turned towards Elena. "Now please show us how to integrate with this crystal here."

"It's a simple process," said Elena. "Hold out the crystal close to you, and try visualizing your strength flowing into it."

Kieran held the crystal close to him, trying to follow Elena's instructions. He did not know what visualizing strength was meant to be, but he just meditated and focused on his wish of always wanting something similar to this. After nearly a minute, the weight on his hands suddenly vanished, leaving behind no trace of the crystal. His sister did not have the crystal either. He could hear the sounds of clapping from the others who were present in the arena.

"With that, you are successfully integrated with the crystal," said Elena. "Now if you wait for a bit, a creature should pop into existence, which will protect you"

With a bright flash of light, two creatures materialized in front of Kieran. The one closest to him looked like a medium sized dragon, but it did not have wings. The other creature resembled a giant armoured snake. That one probably belonged to Kiara, as it slithered to her, checking her out.

Kiara did not show any hesitation in straight up hugging the giant snake. "A giant magic snake for a pet? It's my lucky day!"

Kieran's own Nightshroud was staring curiously at him, as if waiting for something. he stepped closer, reaching out his hand toward his Nightshroud. It hesitated for a moment before taking slow, steady steps forward. Finally, it allowed him to touch it. Its skin felt strong yet flexible, cool beneath his fingertips. Suddenly, a flash interrupted his vision. It looked like Elena decided to bring out her Nightshroud as well.

Elena's Nightshroud was a huge bearlike creature, which Kieran recalled catching a glimpse of earlier. It took a defensive stance in front of Elena, eyeing the new Nightshrouds carefully. Elena slowly ran her hands across its fur, petting the creature to calm it down.

"Don't worry Biero, they are our friends," said Elena. "We will be going on missions with them"

Seemingly having calmed down, Biero started playfighting with Kieran's Nightshroud, as well as Kiara's. Meanwhile Kieran knew he needed to settle on a name fast. He went through a few names before settling on Atsuki, as he liked how it sounded.

"Ki, did you name your Nightshroud yet?

Kiara who was staring at her Nightshroud, turned to Kieran. "Obviously, it is named Viperion."

"It's a name so generic, that only you could have come up with" said Kieran, dodging his sister's look of outrage. Viperion sounded like a name of a ship or something to him, though he kept that thought to himself.

"I will take the name Viperion any day over Atsuki" she replied, heading over to Viperion. Elena, who was talking to Isla and the others, waved Kieran over.

"We shall set out for a mission in a few days. That shall be a good opportunity for you guys to train your Nightshrouds"

"Alright then" said Kieran, watching as the Nightshrouds finished playing, and headed towards them. It was nearly the end of the day, and they had to go back. After saying his goodbyes to Jin and the others, Kieran, Kiara, and Elena headed back to the house they were staying in.

To his surprise, Kieran saw a house in that same street, fully prepared and open. There were a few workers standing there.

"We were instructed to ready this house for Kieran and Kiara," said a worker. "This will be where you two will be staying from now on"

On entering the house, Kieran could see it was an exact copy of Elena's house, but there were two rooms instead of one. Kiara headed to the bigger room, which was located in the corner of the house and claimed it.

"This shall be my room," said Kiara. "You can have the smaller one there."

Whatever, Kieran did not necessarily want a bigger room, so he was fine with it. He planned to be out and about most of the time anyway. Sighing, he headed to his room, and saw it was already prepared with blankets and everything else.

"Wow" said Elena, who was sitting in a couch, watching the others explore the house. "You guys sure do get VIP treatment huh? The quality of the items here seems top notch. I am claiming this as place as my main hangout spot"

Laughing, Kieran returned from his room, and plopped down on the couch next to Elena. "Feel free to stay here whenever you want miss Elena"

"I intend to make full use of this opportunity" said Elena, playfully swatting at Kieran. "Anyway, I need to get going, we shall meet again tomorrow morning"

Elena got up and waved, heading for the exit.

"Goodnight, Elena!" said Kiara, watching as her figure disappeared into the night.

CHAPTER 4

The next few days went by in a flash. It mostly involved Elena showing Kieran and Kiara around the headquarters. Occasionally, they ran into Isla and her friends, but they seemed very busy, barely even present in the headquarters. The leaders were also were missing from the headquarters, with some others standing in for them, people who Kieran did not see earlier.

Kieran used the time to practice with Atsuki, just like Elena did. They were not allowed to fight each other, but solo practice seemed just as helpful. Finally, the day of the mission arrived. They were hanging out at Kieran's place, discussing the plan of action.

"Depending on how well this mission goes, you will get to keep any purple crystals you claim from there," said Elena. "The leaders do have purple crystals at the headquarters, but it is only given after a year of being a member due to how rare it is."

"Do you think we will be able to take out Nightshrouds of purple level," said Kieran. He kept his voice even, but he could not shake off feeling that they would be wiped out instantly the second they were attacked. The Nightshrouds were dangerous creatures after all.

Elena responded quickly. Far too quickly in fact, but Kieran caught the slight tremor in her voice. "Well, the place we are going to is already scouted out by Isla and her group. There is just a single purple crystal Nightshroud there. The three of us should be more than enough to take it out."

Before Kieran could say anything, Kiara started heading towards the door. "Perfect then, let's get the purple crystal's power for ourselves,"

Elena caught her by the arm. "Hold on, we need to get the weapons from the headquarters before we leave."

Kiara nodded, and they started walking towards the headquarters main building. As they walked through the empty halls, Kieran's unease deepened. The air around him was too quiet, all sounds swallowed up by the vast emptiness.

The leaders were not present here, again.

The halls slowly darkened, leading to a corridor of some sort. It stretched before him, dim and uncertain.

"What is this place even?" asked Kieran, taking in the passageway around him.

"Our armoury is up ahead," said Elena. "It was designed this way as a security measure. I should be able to find some spare ANGs over there"

"ANG? Kiara tilted her head. "What's that"

"It's short for Anti Nightshroud Guns," Elena explained "It's very easy to use, so no training is required."

At the end of the corridor, Kieran could see a huge metal door standing in their way. Elena headed to it and pushed it. With a few creaks of protest, the door swung open revealing a room full of guns. Kieran reached for the nearest one, running his fingers across the steampunk inspired design. It felt well balanced in his hands.

"This almost feels like a game" said Kiara, spinning her gun, before pointing it at random objects.

Elena gave Kiara a sharp glare. "If you fire it by mistake, it will blow up this entire building"

Kiara hastily lowered her gun, giving a sheepish smile "Almost forgot this is a real weapon, my bad"

Shaking her head, Elena grabbed a weapon of her own from the wall, and turned towards Kieran, her eyes gleaming with anticipation.

"Come, lets pull off a successful mission for once"

Leaving the armoury, they headed outside. After a bit of a walk, they reached the edge of the headquarter barrier. From the inside, it looked like a translucent wall, shimmering and swirling ahead of Kieran. Kiara gave it a cautious look, keeping her distance from it.

"Go on, the barrier will allow you guys to leave and enter now" said Elena, vanishing out of sight after crossing the barrier. Kieran soon followed, along with Kiara, and they were out of the Eclipse Seeker Headquarters. Kieran could see no sign of entire area even existing.

"Well then, the city barrier is about ten minutes from here, and the raid location is twenty minutes away from there," said Elena in a business-like tone, checking the route on her phone.

"Hold on, let me send you the route." Elena looked through her phone before pausing. "I never did get your contact detail did I, Kieran? I have your sister's as she gave me hers a few days back"

Kieran took his phone out, navigating to the connections page, and held it out to Elena. "Here you go."

Elena quickly added his details to the chat app, and send him the route. Kieran opened to find the route neatly marked on his map. As he glanced at the chat icons, his stomach dropped. His profile picture.

With a jolt of horror, Kieran realised that Elena was now looking right at it.

"Nice profile picture, Kieran" Elena remarked, her voice laced with amusement. "Very creative of you indeed"

Kieran quickly tried thinking of an excuse.

"Yes, his profile picture is very unique indeed" added Kiara, barely supressing her laugher. "I think he forgot that even existed"

Kieran could feel himself turning red. He should have changed it ages ago.

Because right there next to his name, in full display was an old, ridiculous photo of himself, which was edited to look like a warrior, fighting some monsters.

"That's an old photo, I just forgot it was there" stammered out Kieran

Quickly taking out his phone, Kieran tapped the menu panel to change the image. He selected some random flower pictures and set that. He was too late, but he hoped this would help him to prevent further embarrassment.

"Ah, he changed it," said Elena. "Very unfortunate indeed"

"No worries" replied Kiara. "I shall send you the photo if you want."

"Please do," said Elena, barely holding back her laugher.

Kieran sighed. All he could do now was to wait till they forgot about it. Anyway, he had bigger things to worry about. They had reached the city barrier, and they were finally exiting the city for once.

Elena stopped right in front of the barrier. "Now then, we just have a bit more to go. Keep your weapons ready"

Kieran was greeted with the same drab view he saw ages ago. The sky burned through the red desert in front of him. Kieran could feel himself sweating, it was boiling hot. Beside him, Kiara was staring at the landscape outside in wonder. This was Kiara's first time seeing it. This was all new to her, but Kieran had already seen it before.

Their Nightshrouds easily took care of the few shards which did come close.

The sky turned grey, dark clouds gathering in the sky. They all kept firing at the ant, to kill it before it could finish its spell. Abruptly, the creature stopped as if exhausted. It glanced at them with a strange expression and fell over. The sky instantly cleared, returning to the orange sunset which was there earlier.
